Overview ▼

Internet Enduring Material sponsored by Stanford University School of Medicine · Departments of Pediatrics, Psychiatry & Behavioral Health.

This CME activity provides a practical approach to recognizing and managing Pediatric Acute-Onset Neuropsychiatric Syndrome (PANS). PANS is relatively newly understood, under-identified, and under-treated in clinical practice. Infectious and inflammatory diseases can present with severe, debilitating psychiatric and neurological symptoms — and when those connections go unrecognized, clinical treatment may be missed or delayed.

The course covers case scenarios, physical exam techniques for neurological soft signs, treatment algorithms, family education, multidisciplinary coordination, and school accommodations.

What You'll Learn ▼
✓  Recognize early PANS symptoms and presentation patterns
✓  Understand the connection between infection, inflammation, and neuropsychiatric symptoms
✓  Perform physical examination for neurological soft signs
✓  Apply evidence-based treatment algorithms
✓  Coordinate multidisciplinary care across clinical settings
✓  Support family education and school accommodation planning
